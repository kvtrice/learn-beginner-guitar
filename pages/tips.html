Consistency is key. Aim to practice for 15 or so minutes a day. This helps to build muscle memory and keeps you progressing! If you can't squeeze in 15 minutes, even just a couple of minutes a day can help you make leaps and bounds over time.

Keep notice of your finger placement and strumming technique. Perfect practice makes perfect, so you want to make sure you're not accidentally practicing bad habits that may be difficult to reverse in the future. If you're not sure, you can use a mirror or phone to record yourself to keep an eye on your form.

Playing songs you like will keep you motivated over the long term - who doesn't love jamming to their favourite tunes? But try and be careful to choose some songs that are still within your skill level so you can avoid frustration. As you improve, you can gradually tackle more challenging songs.

Timing and rhythm is a skill manyu beginners often over look. It will come with time but you can speed up your progress by practicing with a metronome. Start slowly and gradually increase the tempo as you become more comfortable with the song that you're working on.

üõÄ Take breaks

Believe it or not, there is such a thing as practicing too much! Sometimes it can be all to tempting to keep going when you're passionate about a new hobby, but taking breaks can help keep your focus and prevent injury. This is especially important if you feel any pain, please make sure you stop and rest. It's better to practice a little less today than to risk a longer-term injury that potentially keeps you of the guitar for longer!
